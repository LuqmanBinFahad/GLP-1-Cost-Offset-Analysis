Cost Offset Analysis of GLP-1 Agonists in Type 2 Diabetes: A Retrospective Claims Study and Budget Impact Model

Authored by Dr. Luqman Bin Fahad

An analysis of the 12-month downstream medical cost impact of GLP-1 agonists versus DPP-4 inhibitors for adults with T2DM and obesity.

Key Takeaway: Over 12 months, GLP-1 agonist use was associated with an average reduction of $2,150 in total medical costs per patient compared to DPP-4 inhibitors.

Study Overview & Methodology

This section outlines the retrospective cohort study design. We analyzed claims data to create a robust, real-world comparison between two major drug classes, ensuring the patient groups were statistically similar before evaluating their outcomes.

We aimed to answer a critical question: Does the higher upfront pharmacy cost of GLP-1 agonists lead to downstream savings compared to DPP-4 inhibitors?

  • Data Source: IBM MarketScan® Database (2018-2023)
  • Population: Adults with Type-2 Diabetes and obesity (BMI ≥ 30 kg/m²), newly initiated on a GLP-1 agonist or a DPP-4 inhibitor.
  • Analysis: We used 1:3 propensity score matching on 76 variables (demographics, comorbidities, prior costs) to minimize bias. The final analysis compared 12-month inflation-adjusted costs and cardiovascular event rates.

Key Findings

The analysis revealed significant differences in both total healthcare costs and clinical outcomes over the 12-month follow-up period. Use the buttons below to explore the primary and secondary endpoints.

Robustness Check: E-Value

The E-value was 2.5, suggesting that an unmeasured confounding variable would need to have an association of at least 2.5 with both the treatment and the outcome to explain away the observed cost savings.

Falsification Endpoint: Hip Fracture

There was no statistically significant difference in the rates of hip fractures between the two groups, increasing confidence that the observed findings are not due to systemic bias.
